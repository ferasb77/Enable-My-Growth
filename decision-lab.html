The Problem

Decisions rarely unfold under stable conditions.

Yet most leadership training is designed as though they do.

Real organizations experience a continuous stream of disruption that reshapes the context in which decisions are made. Leadership training that ignores these forces prepares people for a world that does not exist.

01 Changing priorities mid-project
02 New information that arrives too late
03 Stakeholder turnover at critical moments
04 Time pressure that shortens reflection
05 Budget changes that alter what is possible
06 Uncertainty that increases as decisions approach

Changing Conditions

Nothing stays the same.

Throughout the laboratory, conditions change without warning. Each shift creates new constraints, new information, and new pressures that test whether earlier decisions still hold.

Budget Reduction
Available resources shrink after commitments have been made.
Executive Departure
A key decision-maker exits, changing both authority and direction.
Customer Escalation
External pressure arrives with urgency that cannot be ignored.
Supplier Failure
A dependency collapses, removing an assumed path forward.
Media Attention
The decision becomes visible in ways that change what is possible.
New Regulation
Compliance requirements shift the boundaries of acceptable action.
Time Compression
The deadline moves. The time available for reflection disappears.
Conflicting Information
Two credible sources contradict each other at the worst moment.

What We Don't Do

This is not an assessment.
It is not a competition.

We do not
  • Teach one "correct" decision framework
  • Score participants against a benchmark
  • Rank teams or compare performance
  • Assess individual leadership capability
  • Judge decisions using hindsight
  • Declare winners or identify failures
We do
  • Create realistic organizational decision environments
  • Capture the decision journey as it unfolds
  • Reconstruct reasoning from evidence
  • Encourage structured, facilitated reflection
  • Surface patterns that are invisible in the moment
  • Support learning through reconstruction, not judgment
