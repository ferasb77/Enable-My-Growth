Advisory

Structured challenge for leaders facing important decisions.

Not coaching in the conventional sense. A thinking partnership — for people who want to examine their situation more carefully before they commit to a direction.

How I Work

Three areas of engagement

Executive Advisory

One-to-one support for executives navigating high-stakes challenges where clear thinking matters most.

  • Complex decisions and strategic choices
  • Organizational change and transition
  • Leadership transitions and new roles
  • Stakeholder challenges and dynamics
  • Personal leadership effectiveness
Leadership Development

Programs and interventions designed to strengthen leadership capability and decision quality — for individuals and groups.

  • Assessment-based development
  • Leadership group programs
  • High-potential development
  • Team effectiveness work
  • Leadership system design
Organizational Consulting

Supporting organizations in improving leadership effectiveness, assessment practices, and the talent systems that shape who leads and how.

  • Assessment practice design
  • Talent strategy and succession
  • Leadership culture diagnosis
  • Decision quality improvement
  • Organizational effectiveness

This is not therapy. It's not cheerleading.

Most advisory relationships promise support, encouragement, and accountability. Mine offers something different: a serious examination of the situation before conclusions are drawn.

That means asking questions that are sometimes uncomfortable. Challenging explanations that have been accepted too quickly. Examining assumptions before they harden into decisions. And resisting the pressure to reassure when what the situation actually requires is clearer thinking.

The goal is not to feel better about a difficult situation. The goal is to understand it better — and from that understanding, make better decisions.
