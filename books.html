The Inner Conversation

Every leader carries an internal narrative — about who they are, what people expect of them, what risks are acceptable, and what failure means. These narratives are not always visible, even to the person holding them.

They shape what information gets noticed. Which options get considered seriously. How uncertainty is tolerated. And what kinds of decisions feel possible at all.

The Inner Conversation explores the assumptions, narratives, and internal dialogues that shape human judgment — and what becomes possible when those assumptions are examined rather than simply acted upon.

A book for people who lead, decide, and influence — and who want to understand the conditions shaping how they do each of these things.
