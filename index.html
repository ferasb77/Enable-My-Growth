What if the accepted explanation is incomplete?

Most leadership advice focuses on what people should do. My work focuses on understanding why people do what they do in the first place.

Across leadership development, assessment, organizational consulting, and research, I explore a recurring question: the patterns that form before action, the conditions that shape commitment, the assumptions that arrive before evidence.

Organizations explain performance through personality. Leaders explain hesitation through motivation. Teams explain disagreement through resistance. In many cases, the explanation is accepted before the conditions have been examined.

My work is built around challenging that tendency.
